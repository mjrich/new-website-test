Getting there

Our venue, Earthtrek Expeditions, is located in Lotus, California, which is about 50 miles east of Sacramento. It will take about 1 hour to drive from Sacramento to Lotus, California. There will be plenty of parking available on-site.

For those flying in, the best airport is Sacramento International Airport (SMF), which is about a 15 minute drive away from downtown Sacramento and an hour drive from Lotus, California. Oakland International Airport (OAK) and San Francisco International Airport (SFO) are both about 1.5 hours away from Sacramento and 2.5 hours from Lotus.

In addition, Sacramento has Amtrak (train), Greyhound bus and Megabus stations. It is also has Lyft and Uber.

For those driving, we suggest you carpool to the site, either by riding with local friends with cars or renting your own cars in small groups.

For those in need of a ride from Sacramento, please email Max or ThienVinh by July 15th if you want our help arranging a carpool ride to and from Lotus. Otherwise, an Lyft/Uber costs about $90 one-way between Sacramento and Lotus.

Tent Cabin Glamping Goodness

We’ve reserved 120 bunk beds in tent cabins at the wedding venue. These are provided at no cost. Each tent sleeps 2 to 12 and we will be assigning people to bunks. Our wedding will also be kid-friendly, though please keep a close eye on them as we are on the river. Please bring your own bedding, otherwise you can rent a sleeping bag for $15 and a pillow for $5 for the whole weekend. You can also pitch your own tent on the venue site as well. If you prefer to stay elsewhere, there are also B&Bs and hotels in the area. We’d love for you to take advantage of the tent cabins, but do let us know if there are ways we can help ensure the tent cabins or other nearby accommodations allow you to fully enjoy the party.

For more information about the venue and the tent cabins, check out the EarthTrek weddings page.

Up a couple steps from the camp entrance, there is a building with half a dozen complementary hot showers and bathrooms with flush toilets available for use at any time. Please use them conscientiously by limiting water usage and only flushing toilet paper. There is also a port-o-potty near the entrance which is step-free and wheelchair accessible. Phone service works well for Verizon and T-Mobile (sorry AT&T customers) but there is also free camp WiFI and charging stations around.
